OpenOpportunity — Style Guide

v1.0 · July 2026 · shadcn/ui + Tailwind CSS visual language reference for future design updates

1. Brand principle

OpenOpportunity is a job portal first, but every screen should quietly surface two more doors: startup partnerships and community opportunities. Visually this means: keep the core job-search experience clean and corporate (familiar to Indeed/LinkedIn/Naukri users), and use two consistent accent colors — amber for partnerships, teal for community — so users learn to recognize those paths anywhere on the site.

H1 / 32–52px / 800Aa Job title

Body / 14–15px / 400Aa Body copy for descriptions and content
Meta / 12–13px / 600Aa Timestamps, counts, labels

4. Spacing & grid

Spacing scale (px): 4 · 8 · 12 · 16 · 20 · 24 · 32 · 48 · 64. Card padding 20–28px. Section vertical rhythm 56–64px on desktop, 32–40px on mobile.

Layout container: max-width 1120–1280px, centered, 24px side padding. Grid: repeat(auto-fit, minmax(240–300px, 1fr)) for card grids so columns collapse automatically on narrow viewports without separate mobile layouts. Two-column pages (content + sidebar) use a 300px fixed sidebar that stacks below content under ~900px.

5. Border radius & elevation

Radius: buttons/inputs 8–9px · cards 12–16px · pills/badges/avatars 999px (full). Borders: 1px solid #E2E5EA on all cards and inputs — avoid heavy shadows; use border + very subtle shadow only on hover or elevated surfaces (modals, dropdowns).

8. Responsive rules

Breakpoint reference: below ~900px, sidebars stack below/above main content and multi-column grids collapse via auto-fit. Below ~900px, header nav collapses into a hamburger menu. Never drop below 44px touch targets or 24px+ body text on any dense mobile list.
